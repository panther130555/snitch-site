1. What the bot does

This bot:

  1. Joins a voice channel when an authorised member runs the appropriate command.
  2. Records audio from every speaker in that channel.
  3. Transcribes the audio to text using OpenAI's Whisper (running locally on the operator's machine).
  4. Saves both the audio and the transcript to disk on the operator's machine.
  5. Uploads the audio and transcript to third-party file hosts (currently catbox.moe and litterbox.catbox.moe) to generate shareable links.
  6. Posts those links into the Discord channel where the recording was started, and optionally mirrors them to a configured "backup channel".
  7. Detects configured "filter words" in real time and pings a configured role with the offending phrase and an audio clip.
  8. Stores per-server configuration data (prefix, filter words, role rules, blacklists, etc.).

A full feature list is available via the ,help command in any server the bot is in.

3. Recording consent — read this

Recording private voice communications is a serious legal matter and the rules vary by jurisdiction.

  • In many jurisdictions ("one-party consent"), recording is lawful if at least one participant consents (typically the person running the command).
  • In other jurisdictions ("all-party consent" — examples include California, Florida, Massachusetts, Washington in the United States; most of the European Union under GDPR; and others), every participant must consent.
  • It is your responsibility to know the law in your jurisdiction and to obtain consent accordingly. The bot operator provides this software as-is and is not liable for unlawful use.

If you do not consent to being recorded, leave the voice channel immediately. The bot does not have any technical way to selectively exclude individual speakers.
